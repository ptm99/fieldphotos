Typical climate change vulnerability assessment steps

  • Scoping
  • Baseline assessment
  • Impact assessment
  • Assessing adaptive capacity
  • Scoring vulnerability

1. Scoping

Scoping of:

  • Specific infrastructure system (eg river protection; drainage; main road)
  • Geographic area covered (eg entire catchment, town or specific site)
  • Linked natural and social system components and assets (eg agricultural fields, market, school, houses)
  • Scoping is needed at key points in the assessment and planning process
  • Scoping is part of priority setting in situations of scarce resources
  • Need criteria to guide the scoping decisions

2. Baseline assessment

  • Baseline assessment of:
    • Status and condition of infrastructure assets
    • Key issues and trends in natural, social, economic and built systems in the area.
    • Past extreme events and impacts
    • Past “adaptation” to extreme events (adaptation audit)
  • Projected climate and hydrological threats
  • Mapping results using GIS (may require topographical survey)
